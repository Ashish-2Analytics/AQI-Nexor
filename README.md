## 📖 Executive Summary
**AQI-Nexor** is a high-frequency analytical study of atmospheric conditions across six major global metropolises. By processing a massive temporal dataset of **52,560+ hourly records**, this project decodes the complex relationship between industrial emissions, weather patterns, and public health. 📉💨

### 🏆 The Achievement
> [!IMPORTANT]
> **Datathon Runner-Up:** This project secured a podium finish for its robust **ETL Pipeline**, precision in handling **missing time-series data**, and its ability to translate raw sensor logs into **executive-level insights**. 🥈✨

<hr>

## 🔬 Theoretical Framework & Research Focus

### 1. The Chemistry of Urban Smog ⚗️
I focused on the "Big Three" pollutants that define the Air Quality Index:
* **$PM_{2.5}$ & $PM_{10}$:** Identifying microscopic particulate matter that penetrates deep into the respiratory system.
* **$NO_2$ & $SO_2$:** Analyzing the chemical precursors to acid rain and urban ozone.

### 2. Meteorological Correlation 🌦️
This project proves that AQI is not just about emissions—it's about **Atmospheric Dynamics**:
* **Thermal Inversion Analysis:** Explaining how stagnant air layers trap pollutants during winter cycles.
* **Wind Dispersion Modeling:** Using vector data to predict how pollutants travel from industrial zones to residential sectors.

### 3. Data Engineering Methodology (ETL) 🔢
* **Extraction:** Sourced 1-year of hourly data across 6 global nodes.
* **Transformation:** Implemented **Linear Interpolation** for sensor gaps and **Z-Score Normalization** to handle outliers.
* **Loading:** Structured the cleaned data into a relational schema for high-speed SQL querying.
